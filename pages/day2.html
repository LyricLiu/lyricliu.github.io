YouTube Gaming

Designing new interactions for Youtube Gaming

2/9/2017

Live video as a new media has become one of the most effective ways to connect with audiences. Instead of being silent viewers, audiences are more willing to regard the broadcast as a face to face conversation and express their feelings in real time. Youtube Gaming live is growing, but the interaction between YouTubers and audiences is limited. Here I present a design of youtube gaming interface to introduce my idea upon possible interactions during a live broadcast.

Observation

I begin by directly involving in several live broadcasts on Youtube Gaming and observing how audiences interact with their You Tubers.

I took the following notes:

01. The needs of Audiences

Self-expression (especially emotional expression) | Communicate with the YouTuber | Arouse attention from other audiences

02. The needs of YouTubers

Gain more subscription | Get feedback | Earn some money

03. Existing media conventions

Comment (text and emoji) | Sponsor and Donation (money) | “Shout out” (voice)

Based on my note, I mapped the relationship between those targeted users within possible media conventions and try to envision new methods of interaction.

Insight One

As the relationship between audiences and YouTuber are mostly N:1, the basic approach to facilitate better conversation is to come up with a filter mechanism to turn the conversation during a live broadcast into 1:1.

There are two possible ways to achieve this:

01. Find out communal interest

02. Assign privilege to sponsor and donator

For now, YouTube only adopts the second approach. Other SNS like “Twitter” has some relevant mechanism in the first approach.

But, how about we combine these two approached together?

Imagine this: You are a crazy fun watching a live broadcast right now. You really want to discuss with your favorite YouTuber about her new haircut and you believe a lot of people may want to discuss this as well. But you don’t want to spend a lot of money as a donator to arouse the attention of the YouTuber. You might hope to find a way to let your idea to be supported and then you will have more chance to be noticed.

So, how about we adopt: Donation + Votes

f you donate $2, you will need 300 other audiences to vote for you, before your topic pops up in the video for YouTuber to answer. If you donate $10, you will need 50 votes. If you donate $500, You don’t need any vote at all.

Then, if you are an audience, you can choose between a GOOD TOPIC and MONEY!

If you are a YouTuber, your can get both a GOOD FEEDBACK and DONATION.

Insight Two

“I don’t really want to say something, I just want to express my emotion.”

Let us try an easy but interesting way of expressing emotion:

Just click the emoji that represents your feelings in real time. What you choose will sneak into a huge communal emoji hovered on top of the screen. You see other audiences' emojis moving towards the communal emoji as well. The system will calculate the widely selected emoji in a short time period and reveal it as a communal emoji.

Of course, you can send a super emoji that allow your choice to last long.
